The Beginning

A Garage, Thirty Children, and a Conviction

In 1972, Cynthia and Alfred Devadason encountered hundreds of families living as squatters along the canal banks of Colombo. Children roamed the streets with no school to attend, no safe place to grow. Rather than look away, the Devadasons opened their rented garage — and a movement was born.

The Beginning

1972 — First Steps

Cynthia and Alfred Devadason witness hundreds of families living as squatters along Colombo's canal banks. Children roam the streets with no schooling, no future. The burden to act becomes impossible to ignore.

1972
72
1975
The Good News Club

1975 — A Garage Becomes a School

Operating from a rented garage, the Devadasons launch "The Good News Club" for thirty canal bank children. This initiative sparks The Lanka Vacation Bible School Movement — a flame that spreads across the island.

75
First Fruits

1978 — A Community Transforms

Nine families become believers and are baptised. Over the following decade, the entire community sees housing improvements, employment gains, and strengthened family structures. The proof of holistic transformation is undeniable.

1978
78
1983
Through Adversity

1983 — Resilience After the Riots

Ethnic riots destroy surrounding buildings. Rather than retreat, the ministry acquires a damaged property — a statement of faith and commitment to the communities they serve. Rebuilding begins immediately.

83
Official Recognition

1985 — Government Registration

Word & Deed Lanka is formally registered as a Social Service Organisation, receiving government approval. This milestone opens doors to wider reach and greater accountability to the communities served.

1985
85
1987
The Colombo Centre

1987 — A Permanent Home

With generous support from Woord en Daad (Holland), the rebuilt Colombo Centre opens to serve 300+ children. A permanent base is established — from which the work can multiply across the island.

87
The Work Continues

Today — Growing Deeper

CEO Timothy Daniel leads Word & Deed Lanka into new communities and new chapters. 60,000+ children reached, 400+ villages served — and the conviction that every life matters burns just as brightly as it did in a garage in Colombo fifty years ago.
